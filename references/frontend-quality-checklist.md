# Frontend Quality Checklist

- Semantics: landmarks, headings, lists, buttons, links, and form controls match their purpose.
- Responsiveness: layouts work at mobile, tablet, desktop, and narrow content widths.
- Focus states: all interactive elements have visible focus indicators.
- Keyboard navigation: menus, dialogs, forms, and controls work without a mouse.
- Forms: labels, help text, validation, disabled, loading, and error states are clear.
- Dialogs: Escape, focus trap, return focus, labels, and scroll behavior are handled.
- Images: dimensions, alt text, lazy loading, and source sizes are appropriate.
- Typography: hierarchy, line length, contrast, and scale are readable.
- Spacing: sections, controls, and dense UI surfaces use consistent rhythm.
- CTA: primary actions are obvious and do not compete with secondary actions.
- Performance: avoid heavy dependencies, layout shifts, render blockers, and unnecessary JS.
- Reduced motion: motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.
